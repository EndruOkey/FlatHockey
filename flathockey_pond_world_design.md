
# FlatHockey – Pond Park & Gameplay Design Notes
Author: Ondřej Mendlík (concept discussion)
Date: 2026

---

# 1. Core Vision

FlatHockey is not only a match-based hockey game.
It should feel like a **living hockey place** where players:

- play games
- train
- watch others
- socialize
- customize their gear

The central idea is a **Pond Park world** that acts as:

- social hub
- lobby between matches
- sandbox hockey playground

---

# 2. Pond Park World Structure

## Main Pond

Primary gameplay area.

Mode:
5v5

Characteristics:

- chaotic pond hockey
- spectators around the rink
- bench players waiting
- social interaction

No rigid systems. Players can freely join and leave.

---

## Training Ponds

Smaller side ponds for skill practice.

Examples:
- 1v1
- 2v2
- 3v3

Each training pond has a **physical setup board** allowing players to configure drills.

### Training Board Options

Players can configure:

- cones
- rebound boards
- passing targets
- puck spawner
- goalie on/off
- drill timer
- sandbox mode

### Training Objects

**Cones**
Used for stickhandling drills.

Layouts:
- line
- zig-zag
- circle

**Rebound Boards**
Bounce pucks back for passing and shooting practice.

**Passing Targets**
Targets that count successful hits.

**Puck Rack / Spawner**
Resets drills and spawns new pucks.

### Drill Systems

Optional:
- timer (30s / 60s / free)
- score counter
- best run leaderboard

# 4. Pond Rotation System (Bench System)

Max active players:

5v5 (10 players)

Other players stay on **bench areas**.

### Systems encouraging rotation

No stamina system.

Instead:

**Bench Boost**
Players entering from bench receive short boost.

Example:
- duration ~5 seconds
- acceleration bonus
- edge grip bonus

No top speed increase.

**Auto Invite**
If players wait on bench, active players see hint:

Press F to swap

**Quick Swap**
Bench player enters swap zone and active player presses swap key.

Swap occurs instantly.

**Idle Kick**
Inactive players on ice for ~60–90s are moved to bench.

**Camera Respect**
Camera may slightly highlight bench players waiting.

Encourages friendly rotation culture.

# 5. Puck & Control Concepts

Puck should visually appear controlled on the blade.

Important principles:

- puck must not feel laggy
- puck positioned on stick blade
- angle determined by stick orientation

During turns:

- puck shifts forehand/backhand side

### Manual puck actions

**Middle Mouse Button**
Drop puck intentionally.

**Left Mouse (Charge)**
Push puck slightly forward during charge.

This allows faster skating without full puck control.

# 6. Competitive Arena Flow

Competitive mode differs from pond mode.

Structured and respectful.

## Automatic Team Entrance

Players spawn in tunnel.

Sequence:

spawn tunnel → skate out → blue line positions

Duration ~2–3 seconds.

---

## Blue Line Greeting

Automatic gesture when players cross center ice.

Stick tap occurs naturally during skate.

Very short (~0.5s).

---

## Faceoff Start

Players move to positions.

Ref drops puck.

Entire pre‑game sequence:

~5 seconds.

# 7. Dynamic Pond World

World reflects real time in Prague.

Timezone:

Europe/Prague

### Lighting Phases

06:00 — sunrise
08:00–16:00 — day
17:00–19:00 — sunset
19:00–06:00 — night

Uses lighting presets rather than full dynamic lighting.

---

## Night Atmosphere

Night pond becomes cozy social environment.

Features:

- warm lamp lights
- longer shadows
- visible breath
- slight ice fog

Goal:

cozy winter hockey night atmosphere

---

## Bonfire at Night

Bonfire becomes central visual element.

Effects:

- fire light
- sparks
- crackling audio

Encourages players to gather socially.

---

## Ice Reflections

Stylized reflections from:

- lamps
- bonfire

Not realistic, but atmospheric.
